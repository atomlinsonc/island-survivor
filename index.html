Island Survivor

The Economics of Survival — A Classroom Challenge

📋 Your Situation

You are the sole survivor of a shipwreck. You've washed ashore a deserted island with almost nothing. You have 8 hours of usable daylight each day. Make every hour count — this island is brutal, and most people don't last a week.

📚 Economic Concepts You'll Experience

  • Opportunity Cost: Every hour on one action is an hour you cannot spend on another.
  • Scarcity: Time and resources are always limited.
  • Trade-offs: More food now vs. shelter before the storm?
  • Diminishing Returns: Each extra hour of shelter-building adds less benefit.
  • Investment: Building tools now saves time on every future trip.
  • Risk vs. Reward: Hunting yields more food but risks injury.

🎯 Survival Rules

  • Keep Food, Water, and Health above zero.
  • Storms are coming — watch for warnings and build shelter in time.
  • The island gets harder the longer you stay.
  • Score = Days Survived. Share your score with the class!
🌿 Rumour has it this island holds secrets far stranger than survival... Those brave enough to explore its depths may find something extraordinary. Or something deadly.
