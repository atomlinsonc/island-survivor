Island Survivor

The Economics of Survival — A Classroom Challenge

📋 Your Situation

You are the sole survivor of a shipwreck. You've washed ashore on a deserted island with almost nothing. Each day you have 8 hours of usable daylight to gather resources, build shelter, and prepare for what's coming. Make wise choices — your survival depends on them.

📚 Economic Concepts You'll Experience

  • Opportunity Cost: Every hour spent on one action is an hour you cannot spend on another.
  • Scarcity: Time and resources are always limited — you can never do everything.
  • Trade-offs: Gathering food now vs. building shelter before the storm?
  • Diminishing Returns: Each additional hour of shelter-building adds less benefit than the first.
  • Investment: Spending time to build tools now pays off on every future gathering trip.
  • Risk vs. Reward: Hunting yields more food, but risks injury.

🎯 Survival Rules

  • Keep Food, Water, and Health above zero.
  • Storms are coming — watch for warnings and prepare your shelter.
  • The game grows more complex as you survive longer.
  • Score = Days Survived. Share your score with the class!
